BMW Founders

BMW was founded in 1916 by Karl Rapp and Gustav Otto as a manufacturer of aircraft engines. Initially operating as Rapp Motorenwerke, the company later became Bayerische Flugzeugwerke (BFW). In 1917, it was renamed Bayerische Motoren Werke (BMW). Franz Josef Popp played a key role in its development, while Camillo Castiglioni helped restructure the company in 1922. Today, BMW is one of the world’s leading luxury automobile manufacturers.

Key Accomplishments

  • Founded BMW in 1916.
  • Started as an aircraft engine manufacturer.
  • Expanded into motorcycles and automobiles.
  • Built one of the world’s most premium automobile brands.
